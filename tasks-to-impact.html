Improve Your Sleep with Dr. Matthew Walker’s Protocol

Dr. Matthew Walker, a sleep expert, shares these science-backed steps to enhance your sleep quality:

  • Regularity: Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day to anchor your sleep cycle.
  • Darkness: Dim lights by 50% in the last hour before bed to boost melatonin production.
  • Temperature: Keep your bedroom cool, around 67°F (18.5°C), to help your body’s core temperature drop for sleep.
  • Avoid Caffeine and Alcohol: Limit caffeine 8–12 hours before bed and avoid alcohol, as even one drink can disrupt sleep architecture.
  • Wind-Down Routine: Use meditation or a relaxing activity before bed to reduce anxiety and prepare your mind for sleep.

Optimize Your Diet with Expert Protocols

Explore diet recommendations from leading experts to improve your health and well-being. Click below to view each protocol:

Gary Brecka’s Diet Protocol

Gary Brecka, a human biologist, recommends a high-fat, low-carb ketogenic diet with the 30-30-30 method:

  • 30-30-30 Method: Consume 30g of protein within 30 minutes of waking, followed by 30 minutes of low-intensity exercise (heart rate ~70% of max).
  • Macronutrients: 70-75% fat, 20-25% protein, 5-10% carbs to induce ketosis.
  • Foods to Eat: Grass-fed beef, wild-caught salmon, free-range eggs, avocado, coconut oil, organic frozen veggies (e.g., broccoli, asparagus).
  • Foods to Avoid: Refined sugars, white rice, seed oils (e.g., canola, soybean), non-organic/GMO produce.
  • Hydration: Add a pinch of Celtic sea salt to water for mineral balance.
Bryan Johnson’s Diet Protocol

Bryan Johnson, a longevity enthusiast, follows a strict, data-driven plant-based diet:

  • Calorie Precision: ~1,977 calories/day, measured precisely to maintain optimal weight and health.
  • Plant-Based: Focuses on nutrient-dense foods like lentils, black beans, broccoli, cauliflower, mushrooms, and seeds (chia, flax).
  • Super Veggie Blend: A daily mix of broccoli, cauliflower, mushrooms, garlic, ginger, and vinegar for anti-inflammatory benefits.
  • Supplements: Includes olive oil (1 tbsp), cacao flavanols, and a custom “Blueprint” stack for longevity.
  • Timing: Eats within an 8-hour window (intermittent fasting) to optimize metabolism.
Andrew Huberman’s Diet Protocol

Neuroscientist Andrew Huberman advocates a flexible, science-based approach to eating:

  • Protein Focus: 1g of protein per pound of body weight daily (e.g., eggs, lean meats, fish) to support muscle and brain health.
  • Carb Timing: Low-carb during the day (e.g., nuts, veggies) for mental clarity; higher carbs (e.g., rice, oats) at night to aid sleep via serotonin.
  • Fat Sources: Healthy fats from olive oil, avocados, and nuts for brain function.
  • Hydration: Drink water with electrolytes (e.g., a pinch of salt) first thing in the morning.
  • Fasting: Delays caffeine 90-120 minutes after waking and uses intermittent fasting to enhance focus.

Boost Your Fitness with Expert Protocols

Explore exercise routines from top experts to enhance your physical and mental health:

Gary Brecka’s Exercise Protocol

Gary Brecka emphasizes oxygen delivery and muscle efficiency:

  • 30-Minute Morning Walk: Low-intensity cardio post-protein (30-30-30) to burn fat and oxygenate blood.
  • Breathwork: 3 rounds of 30 deep breaths (Wim Hof-inspired) to boost energy and reduce stress.
  • Resistance Training: 3-4x/week, focusing on compound lifts (e.g., squats, deadlifts) for strength.
  • Cold Exposure: 3-5 minutes in cold water (50-55°F) post-workout to reduce inflammation.
Bryan Johnson’s Exercise Protocol

Bryan Johnson’s routine is optimized for longevity and efficiency:

  • Daily Activity: 1 hour/day of mixed exercise (e.g., hiking, resistance training, HIIT).
  • Strength: High-intensity resistance 3x/week (e.g., leg press, pull-ups) for muscle maintenance.
  • Cardio: 3x/week of Zone 2 cardio (e.g., treadmill at 65-75% max heart rate) for 45 minutes.
  • Flexibility: Daily stretching or yoga to maintain mobility.
Andrew Huberman’s Exercise Protocol

Andrew Huberman balances fitness with brain health:

  • Cardio: 3-4x/week of Zone 2 cardio (e.g., jogging, cycling) for 30-60 minutes.
  • Strength: 2-3x/week of resistance training (e.g., push-ups, squats) for 45-60 minutes.
  • Neck Exercises: Daily neck stretches and strengthening to support posture and brain blood flow.
  • Sunlight: Exercise outdoors when possible to regulate circadian rhythm.

Enhance Mindfulness with Dr. Andrew Huberman’s Protocol

Dr. Andrew Huberman offers these neuroscience-based mindfulness practices:

  • Morning Sunlight: Get 5-10 minutes of sunlight within an hour of waking to reset your circadian rhythm.
  • One-Minute Meditation: Focus on your breath (inhale 4s, exhale 4s) to calm your mind anytime.
  • Visual Reset: Look at a distant horizon for 30 seconds to relax your eyes and brain.
  • Gratitude Practice: Write or think of 1-3 things you’re grateful for daily to boost positivity.
