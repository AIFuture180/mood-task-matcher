Improve Your Sleep with Dr. Matthew Walker’s Protocol

Dr. Matthew Walker, a sleep expert, shares these science-backed steps to enhance your sleep quality:

  • Regularity: Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day to anchor your sleep cycle.
  • Darkness: Dim lights by 50% in the last hour before bed to boost melatonin production.
  • Temperature: Keep your bedroom cool, around 67°F (18.5°C), to help your body’s core temperature drop for sleep.
  • Avoid Caffeine and Alcohol: Limit caffeine 8–12 hours before bed and avoid alcohol, as even one drink can disrupt sleep architecture.
  • Wind-Down Routine: Use meditation or a relaxing activity before bed to reduce anxiety and prepare your mind for sleep.

Optimize Your Diet with Gary Brecka’s Protocol

Gary Brecka, a human biologist, recommends these steps to improve your diet and overall health:

  • Address Nutrient Deficiencies: Get a comprehensive blood test to identify deficiencies, such as issues with methyl folate due to MTHFR gene mutations.
  • Avoid Synthetic Folic Acid: Remove folic acid from your diet (found in “enriched” or “fortified” foods) and opt for natural folate or methyl folate supplements (e.g., 800 mcg daily).
  • Hydrate with Celtic Salt: Add a small pinch of Celtic salt to your water (e.g., sesame seed-sized amount per 250ml) to improve hydration and mineral balance.
  • Choose Bioavailable Nutrients: Opt for methylated versions of B vitamins (e.g., from brands like Thorne or Pure Encapsulations) to ensure your body can use them effectively.
  • Eat Whole Foods: Focus on whole, unprocessed foods to provide your body with the raw materials it needs for optimal health.

Supplement Disclaimer: Consult a healthcare provider before taking any supplements, including methyl folate or B vitamins, especially if you have medical conditions, are pregnant, or are taking medication. Supplements may interact with medications or cause adverse effects if not used appropriately.

Enhance Your Exercise with Andrew Huberman’s Protocol

Dr. Andrew Huberman, a neuroscientist, recommends this weekly exercise protocol for overall fitness:

  • Endurance (Weekly): Do at least 30 minutes of Zone 2 cardio (e.g., jogging or hiking) to build cardiovascular health; aim for 60–75 minutes if possible.
  • Strength (Weekly): Train major muscle groups (e.g., legs, torso) with 3–4 sets of 5–8 reps using heavy weights, resting 2–4 minutes between sets.
  • Flexibility (Daily): Hold static stretches for 30–60 seconds per major muscle group, 3x/week or daily, to improve flexibility.
  • Recovery (Post-Workout): After training, do 3–5 minutes of slow breathing to relax your nervous system and aid recovery.
  • Supplements (Optional): Consider Alpha-GPC (300mg) or L-Tyrosine (500mg) 30–60 minutes before key workouts to boost focus and performance.

Supplement Disclaimer: Consult a healthcare provider before taking any supplements, including Alpha-GPC or L-Tyrosine, especially if you have medical conditions, are pregnant, or are taking medication. Supplements may interact with medications or cause adverse effects if not used appropriately.

Practice Mindfulness with Dr. Julie Smith’s Protocol

Dr. Julie Smith, a clinical psychologist, suggests these mindfulness techniques to improve mental well-being:

  • Grounding Exercise: Take 1 minute to notice 5 things you can see, 4 things you can touch, 3 things you can hear, 2 things you can smell, and 1 thing you can taste to anchor yourself in the present.
  • Mindful Breathing: Spend 2 minutes breathing deeply—inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 4 seconds, exhale for 4 seconds—to calm your mind.
  • Journal Your Emotions: Write down how you’re feeling for 3 minutes to process emotions without judgment.
  • Body Scan: Take 2 minutes to mentally scan your body from head to toe, noticing any tension and consciously relaxing those areas.
  • Gratitude Practice: Name 3 things you’re grateful for each day to shift your focus toward positivity.
