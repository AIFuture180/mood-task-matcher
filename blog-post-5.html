How to Use Exercise to Boost Your Mental Health

Move Your Body, Lift Your Mood

Exercise isn’t just good for your body—it’s a powerful way to improve your mental health. Physical activity releases endorphins, reduces stress, and boosts your mood. Here’s how to incorporate movement into your daily life to feel happier and more balanced.

4 Steps to Boost Mental Health with Exercise

  1. Start with a 5-Minute Walk: A short walk around your block or office can clear your mind and boost your energy. Aim for a brisk pace to get your heart rate up.
  2. Try a Fun Activity: Choose something you enjoy, like dancing to your favorite song or playing a quick game of catch. Fun movement feels less like a chore.
  3. Incorporate Stretching: Spend 3 minutes stretching your arms, legs, and back. This relieves physical tension and helps you feel more relaxed.
  4. Make It Social: Invite a friend for a walk or join a group class. Social interaction combined with exercise doubles the mood-boosting benefits.

Get Moving, Feel Better

You don’t need a gym to benefit from exercise—just a few minutes of movement can make a big difference. Pick one of these steps to try today and feel the mental health boost!
